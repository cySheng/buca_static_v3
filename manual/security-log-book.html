Security

The security log book

Your guardhouse log, digitised. Record car entries as they happen, let BUCA build your weekly security report automatically, and see at a glance how your members are tracking.

⏱ 8 minute read Security committee

What the log book gives you

Every car that comes through the gate can be recorded against a household. From those entries, BUCA puts together three useful things:

  • A live log of car entries you can search and filter.
  • A weekly security report, generated for you automatically.
  • Member insights — who's active, who's new, and who has dropped off.

Logging a car entry

  1. Open the log book

    Go to Security → Log book. The most recent entries sit at the top.

  2. Add an entry

    Tap New entry and type the car plate. If the plate is already linked to a household, BUCA fills in the resident automatically.

  3. Confirm

    The entry is timestamped and saved. That's it — it immediately feeds this week's report.

Plates linked during import

If you added car plates when you imported your data, the log will recognise residents straight away — no extra setup.

The weekly security report

Each week, BUCA totals up the entries and produces a report on its own — no spreadsheet wrangling. You'll see car entries by day, the busiest times, and how this week compares to the last.

Understanding your members

Beyond the raw count, BUCA looks at the pattern of entries to help you read your community at the gate:

GroupWhat it tells you
Loyal membersHouseholds that come through regularly — your steady, active residents.
New membersPlates seen for the first time recently — new residents or newly registered cars.
DropoutsHouseholds that used to be active but have gone quiet — worth a friendly check-in.
