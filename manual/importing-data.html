Importing your data

Moving your association onto BUCA starts with bringing your existing records across. This guide walks you through it from blank spreadsheet to a fully populated database — and yes, real human assistance is provided at every step.

⏱ About 20 minutes Committee admin Assistance included
You don't have to do this alone

If you'd rather we set it up for you, send us your existing spreadsheet and we'll handle the import together. Most committees are up and running the same day.

Before you begin

Importing pulls three kinds of records into BUCA at once. It helps to have them gathered before you start:

  • Households — the homes in your area (address or house number).
  • Members — the residents linked to each household, with a contact number.
  • Payments (optional) — any security fees or membership dues you've already collected and want on record.

You'll need committee access to your association in BUCA. If you haven't created your association yet, do that first — it takes a couple of minutes.

Already using a spreadsheet?

Perfect — that's exactly what BUCA imports. You don't need to start from scratch; you'll be copying your existing columns into our template.

Step 1 · Download the BUCA template

The template is a spreadsheet laid out exactly the way BUCA expects to read it. Using it means your import will line up correctly the first time.

BUCA_Import_Template.xlsxFound under Members → Import → Download template
Where to find it

Open it in Excel, Numbers or Google Sheets — whatever you already use. You'll see one row of headings and a few example rows to show you the format. Leave the heading row exactly as it is.

Understanding the columns

Here's what each column means. Required columns must be filled for every row; optional ones can be left blank.

ColumnRequiredWhat goes here
House NoYesThe unit or house number, e.g. 14 or A-12-3.
StreetYesStreet name, e.g. Jalan Melur.
Member NameYesThe resident's full name as you'd like it to appear.
PhoneYesA contact number, e.g. 012-345 6789.
EmailNoUsed for receipts and reminders, if you have it.
Car PlateNoOne or more plates, separated by a comma.
RoleNoOwner or Tenant. Defaults to Owner if blank.
Don't rename or reorder the headings

BUCA matches your data using the heading names in the top row. If a heading is changed, deleted or moved, that column won't be recognised. Add your data below the headings and leave the top row untouched.

Step 2 · Fill in your members

Copy your existing records into the template, one resident per row. A few pointers that save time later:

  • One row per member. If a household has two residents you want on record, give them a row each — repeat the same House No and Street on both.
  • Keep house numbers consistent. Decide on a format (14 vs No. 14) and stick to it, so BUCA groups households correctly.
  • Multiple cars? Put every plate in the one Car Plate cell, separated by commas: WXY 1234, VBA 88.

Step 3 · Upload to BUCA

Once your spreadsheet is ready, bring it into BUCA:

  1. Open the import screen

    In your association, go to Members → Import. This is the same place you downloaded the template.

  2. Choose your file

    Click Upload spreadsheet and select the file you just filled in. BUCA accepts .xlsx and .csv.

  3. Let BUCA read it

    BUCA scans every row and matches your columns to its fields. This usually takes a few seconds.

Step 4 · Review & confirm

Before anything is saved, BUCA shows you a preview of exactly what will be created — how many households, members and cars — and flags any rows that need a second look. Nothing is imported until you confirm.

  • Green rows are ready to import.
  • Amber rows have a warning (for example, a missing phone number) — you can fix them now or import and tidy up later.
  • Red rows have an error that must be fixed before that row can be imported.

Fix anything you can in the spreadsheet, re-upload, and when the preview looks right, press Confirm import. Your members appear in BUCA straight away.

You can import more than once

New residents next month? Just fill in a fresh template with only the new rows and import again. BUCA adds them alongside your existing members.

Importing past payments (optional)

If you'd like your existing payment history in BUCA from day one, you can import that too. Use the Payments tab of the template and list one payment per row, matching each to a member by their House No and Member Name.

ColumnWhat goes here
House NoMust match a household you've imported.
AmountThe amount paid in RM, e.g. 50.00.
Paid OnDate of payment, DD/MM/YYYY.
ForWhat it was for, e.g. Security fee — June 2026.

Common issues & how to fix them

What you seeWhat it means & the fix
"Column not recognised"A heading in the top row was changed or moved. Re-download the template and copy your data under the original headings.
"Missing required field"A required cell (House No, Street, Member Name or Phone) is blank in that row. Fill it in and re-upload.
Duplicate members appearThe same person was listed twice, or a house number was typed two ways. Standardise your House No format and remove repeats.
Payment won't match a memberThe House No on the payment row doesn't match any imported household. Check for typos or import the household first.
